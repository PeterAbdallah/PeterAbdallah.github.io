Before Riding

  1. Training and Licensing:
    • Obtain the necessary training and a valid motorcycle license.
    • Consider taking a motorcycle safety course to enhance your skills.
  2. Gear Up:
    • Always wear a helmet that meets safety standards.
    • Wear protective gear, including gloves, jacket, pants, boots, and eye protection.
  3. Motorcycle Inspection:
    • Regularly inspect your motorcycle for any mechanical issues.
    • Check tires, brakes, lights, signals, and fluid levels before each ride.

On the Road

  1. Be Visible:
    • Wear bright, reflective clothing to increase visibility.
    • Use headlights during the day and night.
  2. Defensive Riding:
    • Anticipate the actions of other drivers and be prepared for unexpected maneuvers.
    • Keep a safe following distance and position yourself for maximum visibility.
  3. Obey Traffic Laws:
    • Follow speed limits and all traffic rules.
    • Use signals and obey road signs.

Be Ready!!

  1. First Aid Kit:
    • Carry a basic first aid kit in case of minor injuries.
  2. Emergency Contacts:
    • Carry identification and have emergency contact information accessible.
  3. Insurance:
    • Ensure your motorcycle is insured and that you understand your coverage.
